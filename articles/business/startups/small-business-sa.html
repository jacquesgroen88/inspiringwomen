Setting Up a Small Business in South Africa

Admin April 24, 2026

From Idea to Registered Company: The Legal Steps

Turning your successful side hustle into a formal, recognized business in South Africa requires a few specific administrative steps to ensure you are legally compliant and ready to scale.

1. Register Your Company (CIPC)

You can register a private company (Pty Ltd) directly through the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC) website for a small fee. Alternatively, major SA banks like FNB and Nedbank offer excellent, streamlined business registration packages that automatically open a business bank account for you during the process.

2. Tax Registration (SARS)

Once registered with CIPC, your company will automatically be issued an income tax number by SARS. You must file provisional tax returns. Depending on your projected turnover (if it exceeds R1 million in any 12-month consecutive period), you are legally obligated to register for VAT.

3. B-BBEE Affidavit

For Exempted Micro Enterprises (EMEs) — businesses with an annual turnover of under R10 million — a sworn B-BBEE affidavit signed by a Commissioner of Oaths is usually sufficient to certify your status. This is absolutely vital if you plan on securing corporate contracts or government tenders.
